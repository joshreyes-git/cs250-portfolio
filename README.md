# cs250-portfolio

1. How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?
I interpret user needs by focusing on what the user wants the program to do and translating those needs into specific features and tasks. Creating user stories helps because they describe functionality from the user’s perspective, which makes it easier to understand the purpose behind each requirement. This approach keeps development user-focused and ensures the final program actually solves real problems.

2. How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?
I approach developing programs by breaking the project into smaller tasks, planning them in short iterations, and testing frequently to catch issues early. In the future, I want to incorporate Agile processes like sprint planning, maintaining a backlog, and holding regular reviews and retrospectives to improve my workflow. These practices will help me stay organized and continuously improve as I build more complex software.

3. What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?
Being a good team member in software development means communicating clearly, meeting deadlines, and supporting other team members when challenges come up. It also involves being open to feedback and willing to adjust your work to better fit team goals. Overall, a good team member contributes reliably while helping the team stay focused and collaborative throughout the development process.
